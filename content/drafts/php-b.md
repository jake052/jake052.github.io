*Variant B — story-led · [A](/drafts/php-a/) · [C](/drafts/php-c/)*

Try something. Think about the document a stranger would need to support *you* tomorrow. How you communicate. What your tone does when you're tired. Your morning routine, in order, including the small stuff that makes you, you.

Hard, isn't it? Now imagine that document is fourteen months old, the person who knew you best left in March, and tonight's worker has never met you.

That's social care, most days. I know because I spent eight years in it — support worker first, then managing supported living services. And I know because my brother Ollie is supported by it. He can't fill the gaps in his own record. Someone has to build a system that doesn't leave gaps.

## Two dead ends, one direction

I didn't start with the answer. I built a [social story tool](/projects/social-story-builder/) first, because I thought care's documents were the problem. It worked — and the market was already full of people building better documents.

So I built [better note-taking](/projects/care-plan-update-system/) instead, because maybe the problem was what goes *in*. That worked too. And the richer notes just piled up in the same silo as the thin ones.

Which is how I found the real problem. It isn't the inputs or the outputs. It's that nothing in the middle joins them up. A note that says *"settled well at bedtime"* and a plan that says *"struggles to settle"* can sit in the same system for a year, three feet apart, and never meet.

## The living picture

Intelligence agencies fuse satellites, patrols, and intercepts into one operating picture because no single source tells the truth alone. People Helping People does that for a person's care: every note, assessment, observation and family comment feeding one living model — kept current as new information arrives, honest about how confident it is, and able to ask a human when two sources disagree.

For Ollie, that's the difference between support that reacts to him and support that *knows* him.

## Where I am, honestly

Two working prototypes on synthetic data, the fusion layer in design, and two questions I refuse to rush: what this should cost, and how you build AI that writes about people who can't audit what's written. The people the system describes will help design it, or it isn't worth building.

{{< annotation >}}Every person deserves to be known by the people who support them.{{< /annotation >}}
